General Game Info

  • GAME CATEGORY: Traditional(?) Fighter
  • DEVELOPER: Twilight Frontier, Team Shanghai Alice
  • PRICE: $24.99

Inputs

Melee

A

Melee is one of the three main attack buttons and allows for melee attacks.

Shot

B

Shot is one of the three main attack buttons and allows for basic projectile attacks.

Skill

C

Skill is one of the three main attack buttons and allows for more utility moves and projectiles.

Tag

E

Tag allows you to switch between your Master and Slave.

Dash

D

Dash allows for easy dashing, as well as serving as the input for double jumping and barrier guarding.

Melee + Shot

A + B

Melee + Shot is the input for your Occult Powers.

Shot + Skill

B + C

Shot + Skill is the input for your Spellcards.

Skill + Tag

C + E

Skill + Tag is the input for your Last Word.

Game Mechanics

General

Master/Slave

E

15.5 allows you to fight with two characters—your "Master", or your main character, and your "Slave", or secondary character. They each share the same health bar, and thus do not have separate health. In general, your Master character has access to more options and abilities than your Slave. Switching between your Master and Slave is done with the Tag button.

Using your Slave character is called Complete Possession.

Lanes

Unlike other 2D fighting games, 15.5 has 3 different "lanes" that can be moved between—essentailly, you're able to "jump" up and down. Moving to the upper lane will float you up then back down to the center, and vice versa for moving down.

Certain moves have different properties depending on the lane you're in, and some can only be performed in the center or upper/lower lanes.

Resources

White Health

White health is a type of health that remains on your health bar when taking damage in any way.

Slave characters cannot recieve white health; instead, they will slowly turn white health into normal green health while they are active. Additionally, Slave characters will treat white health as normal health, taking damage to white health until it is depleted.

Mana Orbs

Mana Orbs are a resource for certain moves and actions that

Performing any move that isn't a Melee attack, Spellcard, or Last Word takes 1 Mana Orb and cannot be performed if no Mana Orbs are available. Barrier guarding also consumes Mana Orbs.

Occult Meter

Your Occult Meter is a meter that is tied to your Slave character, displayed on your Slave's icon below your health bar. You start each match with it 50% full, and can gain Occult Meter by simply landing attacks (represented by pink H icons collected from your opponent). Your Occult Meter will also automatically fill up over time when not in Complete Possession, however it will not do so past 50%.

You can switch to your Slave character at any time; however, if the meter is red with an X over it, you're unable to switch under any circumstance.

Spell Meter

Your Spell Meter is displayed at the bottom corner of the screen and is used for one thing and one thing only: Spellcards. There are two bars to the Spell Meter; the first bar being as much as the cost of your Master's Spellcard, and the second being as much as the cost of your Slave's.

The more expensive the Spellcard, the longer the character's portion of the Spell Meter. You cannot gain Spell Meter beyond the combined maximum of the two character's Spellcard costs, meaning the total Spell Meter you have varies depending on character and Spellcard.

Movement

Floating

or

Not quite walking in aerial combat, floating is a slower center lane movement option that is primarily used for spacing. It's about as standard as you can get with movement.

Dashing

or + D

Dashing is your basic quick movement mechanic. Almost every character dashes in the same way: moving in a straight line forward. Dashing can be performed in all lanes, though you're able to dash for less time in the upper/lower lanes before you're forced to stop.

Dashing is highly useful for dealing with zoning due to it having graze properties.

Backdashing

or + D

Backdashing is a quick movement backwards, and much like dashing, it has graze properties. However, it cannot be sustained and your character will only hop backwards once before stopping. Like dashing, backdashing can be performed in all lanes.

It also is not invincible, meaning you can still be hit by Melee attacks while backdashing.

Jumping

// or //

Jumping is your ability to move to the upper/lower lanes. You can jump up or down, and jump forwards or backwards.

Jumping has graze properties during the jump, however loses those properties once the apex of the jump has been reached.

Double Jumping

// + D or // + D while out of the center lane

Double jumping allows you to move more freely in the upper and lower lanes. Double jumping in the same direction as the lane you're in (i.e. double jumping up while in the upper lane) will allow you to move higher up in the lane, while double jumping in the opposite direction will do a large jump into the opposite lane (from the upper lane to the lower lane or vice versa).

Like normal jumping, double jumping has graze properties during the jump and loses them at the apex of the jump.

Offensive

Attacking

A/B/C

You have a few types of basic attacks in this game: Melee attacks, Shot attacks, and Skill attacks. Melee attacks can be performed at any time, while Shot and Skill attacks require one Mana Orb, otherwise they won't do anything.

In general terms, Melee is your close-quarters utility, Shot is your long-range projectiles, and Skill is your more powerful utility moves and can be either a melee or projectile attack.

Occult Power

A + B

Occult Powers are special unique moves that every character can use. Using them requires your Occult Meter to be at least 50% full, and using them, whether with the Master or the Slave, will put the Occult Meter on cooldown.

Spellcards

B + C

Spellcards are big, powerful attacks you can perform. What Spellcard your Master uses is chosen on the character select screen, while the Spellcard your Slave uses is predetermined. Each Spellcard costs a different amount, represented by the size of the Spell Meter.

Pressing the Spellcard input while you have enough meter will not perform it, but rather "ready" it. Readying a Spellcard will cause a cut-in, either with just your Master, or both your Master and Slave (if you have 2 full bars of Spell Meter), and allow use of the Spellcard(s) by pressing the same input. While a Spellcard is readied, you are unable to gain any more Spell Meter until you use it.

While readying a Spellcard with 2 full bars of Spell Meter readies your Slave's Spellcard as well, you are not forced to use it and can choose to use only the Master's Spellcard.

Slaves cannot use Spellcards by themselves and can only use theirs from a Spellcard tag attack.

Last Words

C + E

Last Word are highly powerful and expensive Spellcards. They require a full 2 bars of Spell Meter and a full bar of Occult Meter to perform, but can deal an incredible amount of damage if you land it.

Only the Master can perform Last Words, Slaves cannot.

Tag Attacks

+ E (Melee, Lane Melee, Shot, Spellcard) / Any direction + E (Skill)

During most moves, you're able to tag in your partner character to cancel out of the move into an attack from your partner.

Universally, all characters have a Melee, Lane Melee (Melee attacks performed in the upper or lower lanes), Shot, Skill, and Spellcard version of these tag attacks, with each one being performable when connecting with the respective attack type.

Using them requires and uses up 50% of your Occult Meter, with the exception of Spellcard tag attacks, which require 50% meter but will use all of the remaining meter, regardless of how much you had when using it. The meter slowly drains the amount used while fighting as the Slave, but will instantly reduce to 50% (or 0% for Spellcards) when switching to the Master.

When tagging with a Skill attack, any direction can be held to perform the corresponding Skill. For example, inputting + T will perform the tagging character's + Sk.

Throws

+ A (Point blank)

Throws are quick, unblockable attacks that can only be performed when right next to an opponent. Throws can only be performed while on the center lane, and can only grab opponents on the same lane as you; if the opponent jumps out of the way, you'll whiff and be left wide open.

Throw Teching

+ A (When opponent throws)

Throw teching allows you to get out of an opponent's throw by attempting to throw at the same time. Doing so will send you both back to a neutral state.

Counter Hits

Counter hits occur when hitting an opponent during the startup of a move. Counter hits are signified by a glass shattering sound and white action lines appearing around the screen, and deal more damage than a normal hit. Not all moves can trigger a counter hit.

Chip Damage

Chip damage is damage that is recieved while blocking. All attacks deal chip damage, with the exception of Melee attacks. You are unable to lose from chip damage, however you can be reduced to 1 health by it.

Defensive

Guarding

Hold //

Guarding is your basic defensive option. You'll guard against opponent's attacks and take either no or less damage from them. Unlike other 2D fighters, there's no high/low block; instead, there's only one block that covers everything.

Barrier Guarding

Hold D

Barrier guarding is a type of guard that creates more pushback on block, allowing you to get your opponent off you more easily. Barrier guarding will drain your Mana Orbs while it's active, though you can still use it even without any Mana Orbs.

Worth noting that barrier guarding does not negate chip damage. You will take the same amount of chip damage regardless of if you're guarding or barrier guarding.

Blockdash

+ E (During blockstun)

While in blockstun, you can perform a dash backwards to get out of pressure that switches you out to your partner. Doing this in the corner won't push the opponent back though, so be careful.

Pushblock

+ E (During blockstun)

Similarly to blockdash, you can perform a burst out of block that launches the opponent backwards if they're close enough and switches you out. Unlike blockdash, however, pushblock can only be performed by the Master, and it requires at least 50% Occult Meter, and uses all Occult Meter remaining.

Grazing

A mechanic of any Touhou game, Grazing in this game refers to situations where you are completely immune to projectiles and will pass right through them. This state occurs while dashing, or until you reach the apex of a jump in either direction.

Grazing does not mean you're immune to everything though, so you can still be hit by melee attacks.

Teching

/ + A/B/C when recoverable

Teching is the ability to recover early from a combo by pressing any attack button (A, B, or C). Holding forward or back will change the direction you tech in, while holding no direction will make you tech in place.

Tag Teching

/ + E when recoverable

Tag teching allows you to tech while switching to your partner character. Functionally works the same as normal teching, just with the added effect of switching out your characters.
