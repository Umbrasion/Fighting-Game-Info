Universal Moves

Unlike other fighting games, Among Us Arena only truly has one character: the Impostor. As such, all characters' non-Sus/EX moves are completely identical.

Light

Light acts as your quick, weak option with short range. Useful for combo starters, short range disruptions, and as a panic option.

L
  • Fast, literal poke that starts combos.
  • Yummers
L
  • Your fastest low, and starts combos just as well.
  • Slides you forward a short distance.
L (Air)
  • Fast and good for approaching from the air.
  • The Impostor's massive ass gives this move more backwards range.
  • The famous "sex kick".

Heavy

Heavy works well for combos, as well as to launch your opponent. Tends to be slow but more rewarding than Light.

  • H
    • A forward punch that trades off speed for range, making it useful for starting combos and a bit of utility in continuing combos as well.
    • Slides you forward a short distance.
  • H
    • A low punch that reaches far and launches your opponent across the stage. Can be a combo starter at certain ranges, but more useful as a low poke.
  • H (Air)
    • Launches the opponent directly down, bouncing them off the ground and letting this move be a highly effective combo tool.

Knife

Knife is your slower but more disjointed poke and combo tool. Good if you're British.

  • K
    • A downwards knife stab that can start combos if you land it.
    • Can be charged if the button is held down. The charged versions have more damage, as well as becoming an overhead.
    • Has one hit of armor during the move.
    • When hitting an oppoent that's in the air, it will immediately send them to the ground. Doesn't combo but puts you at an advantage.
  • K
    • An upwards knife swing. Very scary, as it's one of your best combo tools, anti-airs, and exceptional at starting combos if you can land it as well.
    • Slides you forward, giving it a large range.
  • K (Air)
    • A burst movement option that rushes you forward. Good for combos and cross-ups.
    • Holding during the move will shorten the distance you travel.

Gun

Gun acts as a combo and zoning tool. Good if you're American.

  • G
    • Fullscreen projectile that hits with the gun first, then the shot. Primarily useful for zoning, but has some combo application.
    • The shot itself cannot hit airborne opponents outside of combos.
    • The shot can be delayed if the button is held down. Gets more powerful if delayed as long as possible.
  • G
    • An upwards gunshot that acts as a larger but slower anti-air. Also useful as a combo tool.
    • The shot itself cannot hit grounded opponents at all.
    • The shot can be delayed if the button is held down. Gets more powerful if delayed as long as possible.
  • G (Air)
    • Very useful for zoning in the air, helped by a recoil effect that pushes you back when the shot is fired.
    • The shot can be delayed if the button is held down. Gets more powerful if delayed as long as possible.

Sus

Sus is your character's unique action that defines their playstyle.

  • S
    • Performs a parry. If an opponent hits you at the start of the move, you will automatically counterattack.
    • The counterattack tracks your opponent's position, allowing it to beat cross-ups and longer ranged moves.
  • S/S
    • Performs a throw when close to an opponent. Cannot be comboed out of, but puts you at a significant advantage.
    • S will throw the opponent in front of you, while S will throw the opponent behind you.
    • Throws cannot be performed in the air.
  • S (Air)
    • Performs your character's unique Sus move in the air. See Characters for more detailed information on what each one does.

Dash

Dash exists solely as way to more easily perform forward/backward dashes, rather than hitting forward or backward twice.

EX Move

EX Move is your character's unique, powered-up action. They can also be performed by inputting Light or Heavy + Sus, however this can be disabled per player in the controls options.

  • See Characters for more detailed information on what each one does.

Super

Super will trigger whatever super you selected before the match. They can also be performed by inputting Knife or Gun + Sus, however this can be disabled per player in the controls options.

  • Super 1 (Right Hand Man's Beam Super)
    • Costs 4 bars of meter.
    • High damage, fullscreen, and can be comboed into. Allows you to end a combo with a large amount of damage.
    • Additionally has invinvibility on startup, meaning it can be used as a reversal.
    • Fires diagonally downwards at a sharp angle when used in the air.
  • Super 2 (Sabotage Install)
    • Costs 2 bars of meter. If this super is used with 3 or 4 bars of meter available, it will consume the additional bars to extend the duration of the install.
    • Allows you to enter a state where you deal more damage, your combos can last longer, and your opponent cannot burst.
    • The duration of the install is indicated by an outline around your healthbar area.
    • Can be used in the middle of a combo.
  • Super 3 (Megaphone Report)
    • Costs 2 bars of meter.
    • Sends out a large shockwave that, if it hits your opponent, will cause them to be launched back with a large slowdown effect.
    • Can be used during any move, and cancels out of the rest of the move, allowing you to extend a combo further or safely get out of the commitment of a risky move.

Taunt

Taunt performs a funny little move. Usually has little actual practicality.

  • Most taunts give you a very tiny amount of meter on use.
  • Taunts can also be performed by rapidly hitting 3 times.
