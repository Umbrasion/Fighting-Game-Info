General Game Info

  • GAME CATEGORY: Traditional Fighter
  • DEVELOPER: Arc System Works
  • PRICE: $49.99

Granblue Fantasy Versus: Rising takes all your favorites from Granblue Fantasy and pits them against each other! Serving as an enhancement to the original Granblue Fantasy Versus, this version tends to be more ground-focused, with more moderate speeds and many design considerations for beginners. Game Mechanics

Resources

SBA Gauge

Your SBA Gauge is your primary meter for your more powerful actions, such as Ultimate special moves and Skybound Arts. It is gained throughout the match by landing attacks, blocking attacks, or moving forward.

The SBA Gauge is located just under your health bar, with a percentage indicator to show how much has been built up. It will brighten when hitting 50%, and turn yellow when hitting 100%, or max capacity. It will also turn cyan when your Super Skybound Art is available.

See Ultimate Skills, Skybound Arts, Super Skybound Arts, and Raging Chain for info on moves that use Bravery Points.

Bravery Points

Bravery Points are a limited resource that can be spent through various moves. You start out the match with 3, and cannot regain them unless you use any Skybound Art (you do not have to land them, only perform them).

In addition to spending Bravery Points on certain moves, they can also be lost if you are hit with any Skybound Art.

If you have less than 2 Bravery Points remaining, you will be in a state called Bravery Penalty where you take additional damage from attacks.

See Raging Strike and Brave Counter for info on moves that use Bravery Points.

Inputs

How to read inputs

Light Attack

Light Attacks are quicker, safer, and generally low-risk, low reward. They can start and continue combos well, and are usually pretty short ranged.

Light Attacks when done in the air will always last until your character hits the ground.

Medium Attack

Medium Attacks are usually your longer-reaching but slightly slower attacks compared to Light Attacks. Usually these attacks offer a bit more utility as well.

Heavy Attack

Heavy Attacks are the slowest and strongest moves, and usually serve as mid-distance pokes or combo extenders.

Unique Action

Unique Actions differ wildly depending on character, with them being anything from a projectile to a buff to just a normal attack. However, a character's Unique Action is likely an important tool in their kit with good application towards their playstyle.

See individual character pages for more specific info.

Skill

Skill is an input that offers simplified ways of performing special moves. For example, if a move would require a input to perform, it can usually instead be performed by just pressing the Skill button.

Block

Block, as the name suggests, offers a way to automatically block by holding a button rather than holding backwards to do so. It also acts as the input for Cross Overs and Evades.

The Block button will automatically turn your character around to block, however it will not automatically switch from standing to crouching, and vice versa.

Throw

Throw performs your charater's throw, an unblockable move that you must be right next to your opponent to perform. Throws can also be performed backwards by holding backwards when pressing Throw.

If your opponent throws you, pressing any attack button or Throw will escape it.

Throws can be performed in the air.

Throws can also be performed by inputting L + M or L + U.

Dash

Dash acts as a simple input to immediately make your character start running.

M + H

M + H (Medium Attack + Heavy Attack) is the input for your Bravery Point actions.

See Bravery Points for more info.
